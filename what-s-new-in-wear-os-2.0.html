What's New in Wear OS 2.0

Released a few months ago, the Wear OS 2.0 update brought a number of changes to the way you use your watch. It redesigned the notification centre and quick settings panel and the Google Assistant and Fit are now just a swipe away. So let's go over the main changes

 

Notification Centre:

 

Your notifications are still accessed by swiping up from the bottom of the watch however, they have been redesigned to allow more notifications to be shown at a time. Taping on a notification will expand it to show more information and depending on the app, will also display a quick action.

 

Google Assistant:

 

The Google Assistant is still accessible by holding the power button and hotword, but can also be accessed by swiping left. It shows three quick actions you can use with the assistant plus the date and current temperature in your location.

 

Google Fit:

 

Google fit is now accessible with a swipe to the left. It gives you a glance at your heart points, activity points and displays a button that allows you to start a workout. Steps, distance travelled and calories burned can be see by tapping on this view.

 

Quick Settings:

 

Quick settings has been given mostly cosmetic changes with the exception of the now playing button displayed at the bottom of the panel. It allows you to quickly play or pause your music and a tap displays full music controls.

 

Watch Specific Notes:

 

Due to the aging hardware used in the Huawei Watch it is no longer receiving system updates. This not an issue for the most part as features are usually added using the Wear OS app updated through the Play Store. As a result of this the Huawei Watch remains on Android 7.1.1 and continues to use the Android Wear Branding. This also means it lacks the system-wide black theme seen in the newer watches.
